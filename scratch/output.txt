## Department at a Glance

The Mechanical Engineering Department of IIT Jammu was established in 2016 and is one of
the three founding departments of the Institute. The department strives to play a role
in nation-building by training engineers in an effort to transform them into future
leaders. We conduct translational research to enable us to surmount technological
barriers so that our present and future needs are addressed. The department offers
B.Tech., M.Tech., and Ph.D. programs. It currently has 19 faculty members, 6 staff
members, and approximately 150 undergraduate students (I – IV year), 28 postgraduate
students (I – II year students of Thermal and Energy Systems Engineering), and 38 Ph.D.
students.

MSc & MS(R) Chemistry
About the Programme
The department of chemistry would like to launch its PG program, i.e. MSc & MS(R) from
academic year, 2022-23. The admissions to this PG program will be done through the Joint
Admission (JAM) entrance examination.
The main objective of the MSc & MS(R) Chemistry program is to absorb the best talent
(20 students in total) after their bachelor degree across the country. The IIT Jammu PG course in
Chemistry will provide them the necessary fundamental knowledge in both, theory and laboratory
practical levels. In addition to a strong foundation in all disciplines of chemistry, adequate exposure
to all analytical instruments in the state-of-the art instrument facility set up by IIT Jammu will be
provided. Given the expertise of the faculty in the Department presently, the students will have a
choice of cutting edge research in chemistry and related inter-disciplinary areas of research. The
program is envisioned to have core chemistry courses in the first year. All the admitted candidates
will undergo a common curriculum for the first two semesters that will cover the core chemistry
courses which would lay the foundation to the elective/specialized courses. The core courses are
designed in such manner that the students could qualify for national fellowship positions. Each
student will be assigned to a research supervisor based on an evaluation mechanism at the end of
the first year. Also, the interested top 5-students will be offered the MS(R) program based on their
performance in the first year. The second year is designed with specialized courses (both
introductory and advanced) as electives and a research component. The MS(R) students will be
promoted to the third year to continue their research with the respective supervisor. The MS(R)
students will submit a dissertation after their program and will be encouraged to publish their
research. In addition, the MS(R) students will have the option to convert to the PhD program
seamlessly with a fellowship from the institute.

Its permanent campus, spread across 400 acres in Jagti, Jammu, provides a modern environment designed to nurture creativity, research, and holistic student development.

IIT Jammu offers undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral programs across engineering, sciences, and humanities. With strong emphasis on interdisciplinary research, the institute is advancing work in frontier areas such as renewable energy, quantum technologies, advanced materials, artificial intelligence, and sustainable cooling. Supported by more than 100 national and international collaborations, IIT Jammu is steadily shaping itself into a globally connected center of excellence and a driver of innovation for the future.

## Research Support Service

Research Support Services are here to help researchers at every step of their work, from finding resources to managing data and publishing. At IIT Jammu, these services include access to e-resources, tools for checking plagiarism, and software for managing references. The library also offers support for citation analysis, research data management, and guidance on intellectual property rights. Workshops on research methods and publishing are regularly organized to help researchers stay updated. These services are designed to make research easier and more efficient, so researchers can focus on what they do best.

To publish your work, select a journal that fits your topic and review its submission guidelines. Write a clear and organized manuscript with sections like an abstract, introduction, and results, ensuring it follows the journal's rules. Submit it through the journal's online system, go through the peer-review process, make any necessary revisions, and wait for it to be accepted for publication. # Academic Profile

Dr. Nilesh Kumar is a dedicated library professional with over 23 years of experience in managing and modernizing library services. Currently serving as the Deputy Librarian at the Indian Institute of Technology Jammu (IIT Jammu), he has played a key role in enhancing library resources and digital access for students and researchers.

He holds a PhD from Babasaheb Bhimrao Ambedkar University (BBAU), Lucknow, with research focused on content analysis of university library websites in India. He obtained his Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.) in Mathematics from Tilka Manjhi Bhagalpur University (TMBU), Bhagalpur. Subsequently, he pursued a Bachelor's and a Master's degree in Library and Information Science from Banaras Hindu University (BHU) and qualified for the UGC-NET in Library and Information Science.

Dr. Kumar has worked with prestigious institutions like BHU, ISRO, and BBAU throughout his professional journey, contributing significantly to library development and information management. He has authored research papers, book chapters, and two books, while also organizing national seminars and workshops on library technologies. His expertise in digital library solutions and passion for mentoring young professionals make him a valuable contributor to the field of library and information science in India.

Apart from his professional commitments, he has a keen interest in poetry and story writing. He is also enthusiastic about taking on new assignments and exploring diverse creative and intellectual pursuits.

and welcomed the first batch of students into the campus in Paloura, Jammu.

and 19 km from the Jammu Tawi Railway Station.

In July 2018, IIT Jammu embarked upon an ambitious journey to simplify its Academic, Research, and Administrative processes (Academic Affairs, Student Affairs, HR, Accounts and Audit, R and C, Procurement, CEP, etc.). Within a year, we built an integrated e-Governance Solution (EGS) to cater to all the processes and workflows with a vision of an almost paperless office. Entire workflow automation is achieved through an organic, bottom-up iterative process with end-user participation.

Over the past six years, our people have recognised the outstanding achievements of EGS in improving institutess ability to create and evolve high-quality software-dependent systems. However, human touch is essential. EGS has demonstrated an exceptional degree of significant, measured, sustained, and shared productivity improvements by composing a symphony of organically designed workflows. EG/SARAL is still a work in progress with evolving processes and new experiments in Academia. True to its name, SARAL helps simplify academic, research, and administrative process logistics and record keeping.

**The Journey**

- e-Governance Solution brought **transparency, efficiency, and accountability** for all stakeholders. Our anytime, anywhere solution has helped us to reduce the paper footprint to a larger extent. During the lockdowns of 2019 and 2020, EGS came in handy in keeping all required office functionalities alive at IIT Jammu.
- We admitted the UG batch 2018 through registrations in the EGS and every admission thereafter. We have maintained every single academic course grade since inception. Course feedback from students and faculty is also available online.
- The HR information is maintained through EGS, including subsequent updates. Faculty and Staff data capture are used for review and progressions.
- In due course, Help Desk (ticketing), leave, scholarships, payroll processing, travel approvals, academic transcripts, grade sheets, admission portals, R and C projects management, including disbursals, reimbursements, and many other administrative processes made their way into this integrated e-Governance Solution.
- Multiple custom workflows have been created with tracking and time limits. Inefficiencies and bottlenecks are addressed through landing page alerts, dashboards,  triggered reports, and Auto-Escalation alerts.

**Achievements**

---

- The list of features provisioned in EGS is huge, along with 140 workflows and several other transactional interfaces. The system is supported by robust reports, dashboards for ready references (summary data), and user messaging channels, including email, SMS, and Telegram integration.
- Processes were defined, matured, and optimized over time. Workflows were defined, iterated, and improved to help us over time. Now, it is fairly easy to add another workflow or report!
- A lot of effort has gone into the design, application building blocks, thought process, and customization with rigour. Our early investment in the e-Governance Solution, a continued thrust, and much-needed support from the administration have helped us to witness a digital transformation of processes - it's right here in front of us. Director IIT Jammu, an exponent of digital infrastructure for institutes, has been very supportive and inspiring and has encouraged us at each stage. EGS keeps the campus and people connected, irrespective of their physical presence.
- While we have successfully implemented our system, we are supporting other higher education institutes in their journey of e-Governance by assisting on the concept, RFP preparation, deployment discussions, and our institutional memory on e-Governance.

## Unnat Bharat Abhiyan

Unnat Bharat Abhiyan is an important flagship program of Ministry of Education, Govt. of India.

## Vision of Outreach – IIT Jammu

The Outreach Program at IIT Jammu serves as a bridge between the Institute and society,
aiming to inspire, inform, and empower students, particularly from underserved,
remote, and border regions of Jammu & Kashmir and Ladakh.

Since 2024, IIT Jammu has hosted over **10,000 school students**
through routine requests, including students from government and far-flung schools. The program seeks to raise
awareness about science, technology, and higher education while fostering curiosity and ambition among students
with limited exposure to such opportunities.

## Master in Science

The main objective of the M.Sc. (Physics) program is to prepare a talent pool of manpower by inducting them right after their bachelor degree and provide them quality education with sound fundamentals to contribute effectively in these rapidly changing times, and in addition train the students to conduct research in a specific stream of interdisciplinary physics. **Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) for Faculty and Project Staff**

---

**Q1. Is it mandatory to take prior approval before undertaking travel?**

Ans: Yes. Prior approval is mandatory for every travel visit. The approval must clearly mention the source of funds. The workflow under 'My Activities → Travel Plan' shall be used for obtaining Travel Plan approval. Select Travel Category as Project in case of project related travel.

**Q2. How can I submit the travel reimbursement claim?**

Ans: After obtaining approval of the Travel Plan and completing the journey, the individual must submit the claim for Travel Reimbursement through the same Travel Plan module under which the approval was taken.

**Q3. Which details are mandatory while submitting the reimbursement claim?**

Ans: Complete journey details must be provided, including date-wise/line item-wise travel information along with DA claimed, hotel stay, and other admissible expenses.

**Q4. Can I claim DA and food expenses if the host has already provided boarding and lodging?**

Ans: No. If the host provides boarding and lodging, no claim towards DA or food expenses is admissible.

**Q5. Can partial support be availed from CPDA, PDF, other projects, or Seed Grant?**

Ans: Yes. Partial support can be claimed from CPDA, PDF, or other projects, provided the same is mentioned while applying in the Travel Plan. For Seed Grant, travel expenses are admissible to faculty only, if the travel is directly related to the Seed Grant proposal.

**Q6. Can LTC be combined with Project Travel or vice versa?**

Ans: No. Combining LTC with project-related travel is not admissible and should be avoided.

**Q7. Can project staff claim travel expenses from CPDA of an individual faculty?**

Ans: No. Project staff cannot claim expenses from CPDA. They can only claim from PDF, research projects, or consultancy projects of faculties, post approval or as applicable.

**Q8. Is submission of the boarding pass mandatory?**

Ans: Yes. Submission of the boarding pass is mandatory as proof of travel. However, if the boarding pass is lost or misplaced, Office Memorandum No. 19030/3/2014-E.IV dated 23.06.2020 may be referred for compliance.

Link: https://doe.gov.in/files/circulars_document/TA_Order_dated_23_06_2020_Eng.pdf

**Q9. Can I book tickets (Air/Train) from sources other than IRCTC, Balmer Lawrie, or Ashok Travels and tours?**

Ans: No. Air and train tickets must be booked only through authorized vendors: IRCTC, Balmer Lawrie, or Ashok Travels and tours.

**Q10. What is the admissible Daily Allowance (DA) for foreign travel?**

Ans: The admissible DA rates for foreign travel can be referred from Notification No.Q/FD/695/03/2000

---

**Q11. Where can I find the detailed travel guidelines and what is the impact of DA enhanced on travel limit?**

Ans: For rates, Notification No.F.No. 3-2/20s06(Admn-I/A&B)/64535 may be referred. For detailed TA rules and guidelines, the document available at the following link should be consulted: https://doe.gov.in/files/circulars_document/TA_Order_dated_23_06_2020_Eng.pdf

**Q 12 Whether self-declaration bills are admissible for travel claim?**

Ans For employees at Pay Level 9 and above, it is mandatory to submit original bills for all admissible expenses (travel tickets, hotel bills, taxi receipts, etc.). Self-declaration in place of bills is not acceptable, except in the case of food/meal bills where actual receipts are not required.

**Q13. Are grocery bills and medicine bills reimbursable through the travel reimbursement module?**

Ans: Ideally the Grocery / Medicine bills are not admissible. However bills related to essential items for field visit may be permissible subject to approval prior to travel.

**Q14. Are we eligible for reimbursement of travel expenses, stay, daily allowance (DA), and other expenses related to travel to hometown or any other place after attending a conference, workshop, or official tour instead of returning to the headquarters?**

Ans. No, the expenses related to travel other than official travel in not admissible for reimbursement.

**Q15. How can we claim reimbursement of expenses incurred in foreign currency during official travel?**

Ans. The process for reimbursement of foreign travel claims is the same; however, as supporting documentation, you must attach a currency exchange letter or a bank statement covering the period of travel for reference.

**Q16. Is the rule for reimbursement of travel expenses for students from a project the same as the institute's reimbursement process?**

Ans. Yes, the institute's reimbursement norms applicable to students will be followed for student travel expenses in respect of their admissibility from project.

**Q17. Whether travel by own car is admissible under travel rule?**

Ans. Kindly refer to clause 2 (iii) and 2(D) of Annexure of OM N0.19030/1/2017-E.IV  dated 13th July 2017Ministry of Finance Government of India.

Inventory management is an important aspect of managing educational institutions like IITs. IIT Jammu has a wide range of inventory items, including laboratory equipment, furniture, computers and peripherals, books, and other educational materials.

The main function of Inventory section is to accurately track inventory levels and keeping accurate records of the goods in stock. Recording all non-consumable equipment/items in the inventory database using E governance provides an efficient way to track inventory levels and ensure that all inventory items are properly cataloged and labeled. Having an inventory database with information on each asset's owner can also help in identifying who is responsible for maintaining the asset and ensuring its proper usage.

To remove old and non-functional items from the inventory, Institute follows the write-off procedure as per Govt. of India guidelines which ensures the inventory is up to date and the institution does not have to allocate resources to maintain or repair non-functional items.

In addition to this, there is a separate form for consumable items (C-form) and non-consumable items (NC form) to ensure proper classification of Assets/Inventory, and employees can view their Assets/Inventory in the ERP system which provides transparency and accountability.

IIT Jammu have rigorous and stringent purchasing policies to ensure fair usage of public funds and to demonstrate transparency to the public.

As per GFR rules, common-use goods and services must be procured through Government e-Marketplace (GeM) and that only registered buyers may make purchases. This helps to ensure that purchases are made from trusted vendors and that due diligence is done to ensure that items available on GeM are indeed procured through GeM. Those Items not available on GeM may be procured through an open tender process.

Procurement process may be lengthy and involve multiple steps, including forming purchase committees, releasing open tenders, and conducting due diligence. It's important to be patient and allow sufficient time for these processes to be completed, particularly for expensive equipment that may take up to a year to arrive after funds have been earmarked for it and the purchasing process has been initiated.

IIT Jammu manage internal procurement requests on E governance portal which provide real-time tracking of procurement requests to ensure timely completion within the designated timeframe.

Overall, IIT Jammu is committed to ensuring fair and transparent usage of public funds through its transparent purchasing policies and procedures.

### About Visvesvaraya PhD Scheme

The **Ministry of
Electronics and Information Technology, Government of
India** in 2014 initiated “Visvesvaraya PhD Scheme
for Electronics and IT” with an objective to enhance the
number of PhDs in Electronics System Design & Manufacturing
(ESDM) and IT/IT Enabled Services (IT/ITES) sectors in the country.

The objective of Phase-I of
Visvesvaraya PhD scheme (budget of Rs.466.00 Crores) was to support
3,000 additional PhD scholars (1,000 Full-Time + 2,000 Part-Time) in
ESDM and IT / ITES sectors. Phase-II of Visvesvaraya PhD Scheme for
9 years, was approved by the Government with a budget of Rs. 481.93
Crores in 2021 with an aim to support 1000 Full-Time PhD candidates,
150 Part-Time PhD candidates, 50 Young Faculty Research Fellowships
and 225 Post-Doctoral Fellowships.

Visvesvaraya PhD Scheme is an
institutional scheme, where PhD seats are allocated to the
institutions and the institutions enroll PhD candidates on these
seats following their admission procedures. Part-Time (PT) PhD seats
are provisioned for professionals working at a reputed
place/organisation. Under this scheme each institution in the
proposal may nominate one faculty for the award for each round of
call of applications for Young Faculty Research Fellowship (YFRF).
Post-Doctoral Fellowship (PDF) is provided under this scheme to
support PDF fellows.

## Counselling

# Page Title: Indian Institute of Technology Jammu | Leading Engineering Institute for Future Innovators

- About Us
- Team
- Services Offered

- Contact Us

In our journey through life, we are constantly striving to turn our personal and professional aspirations into reality. Amidst the pressure to excel, we often face emotional and psychological challenges that disturb our peace of mind.

However, by seeking counseling services, we can address these overlooked aspects of our well-being. The center comprises a team of professional counselors and empathetic student coordinators who can assist you academically and emotionally. Through counseling, you can learn to make better choices, enhance interpersonal skills, build confidence, and improve your educational effectiveness. In one-on-one sessions, counselors provide guidance to explore and express feelings, challenge beliefs, evaluate behaviors, and work towards positive changes.

We, as counselors, encourage you to reach out to us without hesitation for any academic, emotional, social, or financial concerns. Our aim is to help you maintain focus and find happiness.

## When to reach us?

- Stress
- Anxiety
- Depression
- Relationships
- Alcohol/Drugs
- Family Concerns
- Sexual Assault
- Career
- Self-Esteem
- Body Image
- Grades Grief
- Gender/Sexuality

## Services

- Confidential personal counseling
- Workshops and seminars
- Group counseling opportunities
- Referral for management with a psychiatrist, if needed

## The First Visit

When contacting Counseling Services for the first time, individuals can generally be seen the same day or within a day of making their appointment. Individuals coming in for their first visit will be seen for a brief (30 - 45 minute) intake appointment with a counselor who will:

- Provide information about what to expect from the counseling process
- Discuss the individual's presenting issues and concerns
- Develop a plan for either on-campus/online appointment, or audio/video mode, which best meets the individual's needs

## Confidentiality

All counseling records are kept strictly confidential and are not part of campus records. Information is shared only with a person’s written permission. Exceptions to confidentiality are rare and include when a person poses a threat to his/her own safety, or the safety of someone else, or when it is ordered by the court.

The accounts section of an academic institution is responsible for managing the financial transactions and records of the institution. This section handles the financial aspects of the institution's activities, including budgeting, accounting, and financial reporting.

Some of the critical responsibilities of the accounts section in an academic institution include the following:

1. BUDGETING: The section prepares and monitors the institution's budget. It involves working with different sections to identify their financial needs and developing a budget plan that meets the institution's overall goals.
2. ACCOUNTING: The section is responsible for maintaining the institution's financial records. This includes keeping track of all financial transactions, preparing financial statements, and ensuring that all financial activities comply with accounting standards and regulations.
3. FINANCIAL REPORTING: The section prepares financial reports that provide an overview of the institution's financial position. These reports are typically used by management, the board of directors, and other stakeholders to make informed decisions about the institution's financial future.
4. PAYROLL: The accounts section is responsible for processing the payroll for the institution's employees. This includes calculating salaries, withholding taxes, and ensuring all payroll taxes are paid on time.
5. ACCOUNTS PAYABLE AND RECEIVABLE: The section manages the institution's accounts payable and receivable. This involves paying claims on time, invoicing customers, and managing collections.
6. STATUTORY COMPLIANCE: The section makes all statutory payments within defined timelines, including G-TDS, GST returns, income tax, etc.

Overall, the accounts section plays a crucial role in ensuring an academic institution's financial stability and success.

#### Soma S Dhavala

Soma S. Dhavala is an AI researcher, technologist, and educator with over two decades of experience spanning machine learning, statistics, signal processing, distributed systems, and large-scale AI platforms. He has held leadership roles across academia, industry, startups, and social-impact AI initiatives, including at Wadhwani AI, where he led initiatives in agriculture, public health, education, and Generative AI. His work combines deep theoretical grounding in Bayesian statistics, applied mathematics, and machine learning with hands-on expertise in building scalable AI systems and products. Off late, his focus has increasingly shifted toward Frontier Model Development, including LLMs, multimodal AI systems, interoperability frameworks, and reliable AI architectures for real-world deployment, focusing on sovereign and resource efficiency

##### Academic Interests

1. Developing a unified theoretical understanding of Deep Learning and Frontier Models, including questions around representation learning, generalization, scaling laws, optimization dynamics, and reasoning capabilities in large-scale neural systems.
2. Understanding why Deep Learning works from first principles by bridging ideas across statistics, optimization, information theory, dynamical systems, signal processing, and computational neuroscience.
3. Building reliable, trustworthy, and uncertainty-aware AI systems, especially for Large Language Models (LLMs), multimodal systems, and real-world deployment scenarios.
4. Designing scalable AI systems and ML infrastructure for distributed training and inference of GPT-scale and Frontier Models, including efficient architectures, quantization, interoperability, and systems optimization.
5. Developing curriculum and training frameworks for systems programmers and AI engineers that integrate distributed systems, high-performance computing, ML systems engineering, and large-scale model development.
6. Bayesian statistics, probabilistic modeling, approximate inference, and uncertainty quantification in high-dimensional machine learning systems.
7. Generative AI, multimodal learning, retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), and document intelligence systems.
8. AI for Education, Agriculture, Healthcare, and public-interest technology, with emphasis on deployable, scalable, and inclusive AI ecosystems.
9. Explainable AI, interoperable ML systems, and democratization of AI through open architectures, tooling, and platform-centric approaches.
10. Scientific computing, computational mathematics, signal processing, dynamical systems, and their intersections with modern AI.

##### Research Experience

IPR, Monitoring & Evaluation for AI Programs — Indian Institute of Technology Jammu / Central Project Management Unit (CPMU), Ministry of Education (2024–2025) :

Contributed to initiatives around Intellectual Property Rights (IPR), Monitoring, Evaluation, and Learning (MEL) frameworks for AI-focused national programs and innovation ecosystems.

Applied Research in Machine Learning and Generative AI (LLMs) — Wadhwani AI (2020–2024): Led applied research initiatives in Machine Learning and Generative AI with a focus on Large Language Models (LLMs), Document Intelligence, AI for Agriculture, Public Health, and Education. Worked on scalable AI systems, retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), synthetic data generation, uncertainty-aware inference, and production-grade AI deployment for real-world impact.

EdTech, AI for Learning Systems, and AI Platforms (Public & Private Sector) — Various organizations including Embibe, EkStep Foundation, NIIT, and independent consulting engagements (2014–2019): Worked on AI-driven learning systems, recommendation engines, learning analytics, NLP-based educational tools, conversational analytics, ML platforms, and scalable architectures for digital education ecosystems serving both public and private sector initiatives.

Bayesian Statistics, Computational Biology, and High-Dimensional Inference — Texas A&M University (2005–2011): Ph.D. and Postdoctoral research focused on Bayesian semiparametric modeling, heterogeneous gene expression analysis, MCMC methods, high-dimensional graphical models, and uncertainty quantification.

Applied & Computational Mathematics, Signal Processing, and Distributed Estimation — University of Washington, Indian Institute of Technology Madras, and Iowa State University (1997–2005): Research work spanning dynamical systems, stochastic processes, deep learning foundations, time-frequency analysis, adaptive signal processing, and distributed estimation in wireless sensor networks.
